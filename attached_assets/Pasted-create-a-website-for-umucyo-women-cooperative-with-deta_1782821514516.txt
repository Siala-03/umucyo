Umucyo Women Cooperative was established in 2022 with the goal of contributing to Rwanda's national development through innovation, technology, and sustainable agriculture. The cooperative is proudly composed of 90% women, reflecting our commitment to women's leadership and economic empowerment.

We operate in the coffee and horticulture sectors, promoting value addition, improving product quality, and creating market opportunities for our members. By embracing modern technologies and sustainable farming practices, we aim to increase farmers' incomes while building resilient communities.

Our cooperative also focuses on empowering women and creating employment opportunities for young women and girls, enabling them to develop skills, earn sustainable incomes, and become active contributors to Rwanda's economic growth. At the same time, we are committed to protecting the environment through climate-smart agriculture, responsible natural resource management, and environmentally friendly business practices.

Mission

To empower women through sustainable agriculture by promoting value addition in coffee and horticulture, creating employment opportunities for young women and girls, adopting innovative technologies, and protecting the environment while contributing to Rwanda's socio-economic development.

Vision

To become a leading women-led cooperative in Rwanda, recognized for excellence in coffee and horticulture value chains, women's economic empowerment, youth employment, environmental stewardship, and the use of innovative technologies to improve livelihoods and create lasting social impact.

Our Services

At Umucyo Women Cooperative, we provide a range of agricultural and business development services that strengthen livelihoods, improve product quality, and promote sustainable economic growth.

Coffee Production and Sourcing

We work with smallholder coffee farmers to produce and source high-quality Arabica coffee. Our cooperative promotes good agricultural practices, quality assurance, and traceability to meet the requirements of local and international markets.

Coffee Processing and Value Addition

We support coffee processing through washing, sorting, grading, roasting, grinding, and packaging. By adding value to coffee, we help increase farmers' incomes while supplying premium coffee products to domestic and export markets.

Horticulture Production

Our members cultivate a variety of horticultural crops, including fruits and vegetables, using sustainable farming methods. We promote year-round production, improved crop quality, and reliable market access for fresh produce.

Farmer Training and Capacity Building

We provide regular training programs on modern farming techniques, climate-smart agriculture, post-harvest handling, quality management, financial literacy, cooperative management, and agribusiness development to improve productivity and profitability.

Women's Economic Empowerment

Empowering women is at the heart of our cooperative. We create opportunities for women to participate in leadership, entrepreneurship, skills development, income-generating activities, and access to markets and financial services.

Agricultural Extension Services

Our extension services provide technical support to farmers through field visits, demonstrations, advisory services, and access to improved agricultural technologies. We help members increase productivity while promoting environmentally sustainable farming practices.

Market Linkages and Export Facilitation

We connect farmers with reliable local and international buyers by ensuring consistent quality, aggregation of produce, and compliance with export standards. We support members in accessing profitable markets and building long-term commercial partnerships.

Our Partners

Umucyo Women Cooperative values strong partnerships that help us empower women, strengthen agricultural value chains, and promote sustainable development. We work closely with government institutions and development stakeholders to improve productivity, market access, environmental conservation, and community livelihoods.

Our key partners include:

· MINAGRI – Ministry of Agriculture and Animal Resources: Supporting agricultural development, farmer capacity building, and sustainable value chain growth.

· RAB – Rwanda Agriculture and Animal Resources Development Board: Providing technical support, research, improved agricultural technologies, and extension services.

· MINEMA – Ministry in Charge of Emergency Management: Collaborating on environmental resilience, climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, and community preparedness initiatives.

· NAEB
